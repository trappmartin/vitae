Research

My research focuses on understanding and utilising machine learning. In particular, I'm interested the deep probabilistic architecture called sum-product networks and related tractable probabilistic models. For learning tractable probabilistic models I explore approaches based on Bayesian learning, Bayesian nonparametrics and deep learning. My PhD is supervised by Franz Pernkopf and in cooperation with Robert Peharz.

During my work I publish all my developed software as open source software packages written in Julia. As a result, I created several open source projects including: BayesianNonparametrics.jl, SumProductNetworks.jl Since spring 2018 I'm a core developer of the probabilistic programming language Turing.jl originaly created and now managed by Hong Ge at the University of Cambridge.
